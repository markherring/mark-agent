# B2B SaaS GTM Diagnostic & Pipeline Analysis
## Sample Work Product (Anonymized)

**Client:** B2B Infrastructure/Observability Company
**Context:** Series C, $50M ARR, 200+ employees
**Engagement:** 2-week GTM diagnostic with full stakeholder interviews

---

## Executive Summary

The pipeline problem is not volume—it's signal quality at the top of the funnel. Website traffic is declining (organic down 10% over six quarters), and the traffic attracted converts poorly (7.5% vs 10% industry standard). This weak signal cascades downstream: loose MQL definitions, low-quality meetings, extended sales cycles (60 → 100 days), and cratering win rates (40% → 15%).

The system is busy, but not effective. Teams are compensating for poor signal with increased effort—more paid spend, more SDR activity, more AE time spent qualifying. This is not scalable.

### What We Found

**Top of funnel is weakening and unmeasured**
- Organic traffic declining while paid increases (working harder to attract fewer people)
- Zero visibility into GitHub, community, or social signals—stated primary demand sources
- Website conversion 25% below benchmark (7.5% vs 10%)

**ICP is undefined**
- MQL→SQL conversion is 75% below industry standard (7% vs 27%)
- Win rates collapsed from 40% to 15%
- Sales quotes: "We don't know who the customer is" and "We're bringing in stuff that's just not converting"

**Execution is suffering from strategy gaps**
- Sales cycles lengthened 67% (60 → 100 days)
- SDRs measured on meetings, not quality—conversion rates unknown
- AEs spending expensive time on early qualification (should be SDR work)
- No tools for economic buyer conversations (technical champions lack budget authority)

**PLG exists but its role is undefined**
- Cloud offering generates revenue but strategic purpose is unclear
- No systematic handoff from cloud users to enterprise sales
- Leadership quote: "I don't know why we have it"

### What's Not the Problem

- **Pipeline volume:** Coverage appears adequate
- **Product-market fit:** 86% renewal rate, Fortune 100 logos
- **Sales competence:** Team is working hard and hitting activity metrics
- **Partners:** Absent, but not a current constraint

### What Leadership Must Decide

Before optimizing tactics, align on strategy:

1. **What counts as TOFU?** Website only, or website + GitHub + community + social?
2. **Who is our ICP?** Not "observability customers"—specific firmographics, roles, budget authority
3. **What qualifies as MQL/SQL?** Current definitions are demonstrably too loose
4. **What is PLG's role?** Lead source? Qualification? Stand-alone growth engine?
5. **What is "good" pipeline?** Define quality metrics beyond volume

### The Path Forward

Fix the signal, not the effort:

1. **Measure your actual TOFU** (GitHub, community, social)—you can't optimize what you don't measure
2. **Run ICP analysis on closed-won deals**—define who actually buys and why
3. **Tighten MQL/SQL definitions**—trade volume for quality (target 20%+ MQL→SQL vs current 7%)
4. **Equip sales for economic buyers**—ROI tools, value frameworks, business case templates
5. **Define PLG strategy**—align Marketing and Sales on its role and metrics

**The opportunity:** At benchmark conversion rates, your current inquiry volume would generate 7.6× more SQLs. You don't need more pipeline. You need better pipeline.

## Key Analysis Sections

### Conversion Performance vs. Benchmarks

| Stage | Company | Industry Std | Best in Class | Gap |
|-------|---------|--------------|---------------|-----|
| Website → Inquiry | 7.0% | 10% | 12% | -30% |
| Inquiry → MQL | 7.8% | 15% | 20% | -48% |
| MQL → SQL | 6.8% | 27% | 32% | -75% |

**The Compounding Effect:** Out of 46,800 inquiries in Q4, only 250 became SQLs (0.5% total conversion). At benchmark rates (10% × 15% × 27%), would generate **1,900 SQLs**—a 7.6× improvement.

### Sales Cycle & Win Rate Trends

- **New business:** 60 days (2024) → 100 days (2025) = +67%
- **Win rates:** 40% (historical) → 15% (current) = -62%

When budgets tighten, bad opportunities die and good opportunities take longer. Experiencing both suggests pipeline contains mostly marginal opportunities that were borderline even in good times.

### Marketing Resource Allocation

| Funnel Stage | % Effort | Implication |
|--------------|----------|-------------|
| New Visitors → Leads | 60% | Heavy focus on volume, low quality |
| Pipeline → Close | 20% | Minimal support for late-stage deals |
| Expansion ARR | 10% | Underinvested despite 40% of growth |

**Why this is backwards:**
- Expansion is 50% more cost-efficient than new customer acquisition
- Expansion sales cycles: 37 days (vs 100 for new business)
- Expansion drives 40% of company growth, gets 10% of marketing effort

## Methodology Notes

**Data sources:**
- Stakeholder interviews (CEO, CMO, VP Sales, Sales Ops, SDR Manager)
- CRM data analysis (6 quarters)
- Website analytics
- Competitive benchmarking

**Analytical approach:**
1. Funnel conversion analysis at every stage
2. Benchmark comparison (industry standard, best-in-class)
3. Trend analysis (6-quarter view)
4. Stakeholder interview synthesis
5. Resource allocation assessment

**Deliverable structure:**
- Executive summary (diagnosis + path forward)
- Detailed findings by funnel stage
- "What's working" and "What's not working"
- Strategic decisions for leadership
- Immediate next steps with metrics
