## Technical Depth Demonstrated

**Event-Driven Architecture (EDA):**
- Publish-subscribe pattern replacing tightly coupled connections
- Systems emit and consume events (small, structured messages)
- Sensors publish once, multiple systems subscribe instantly
- Decoupled, dynamic systems that scale naturally

**MQTT Broker:**
- Core of event layer
- Lightweight protocol for secure, efficient edge-to-cloud communication
- Enables real-time data distribution at industrial scale

**Unified Namespace (UNS):**
- Real-time, structured view of entire enterprise
- Single shared context where all data is published
- Source of truth for people, systems, and AI agents
- Eliminates need for complex point integrations
- Supports convergence of IT and OT domains

**Enterprise-Grade Requirements:**
- Scalability to support growth (thousands of devices, millions of messages/sec)
- Reliability (high availability, fault tolerance)
- Interoperability (connect everything - PLCs, SCADA, MES, ERP, cloud)
- Security at scale (encryption, authentication, governance)
